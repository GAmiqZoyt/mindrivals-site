MindRivals — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

MindRivals ("the app", "we") is a quiz and brain-teaser game. This policy explains what data the app handles and how. We collect the minimum needed to run the game's online features.

1. Data we collect

Account data. Playing online features (leaderboards, duels, Infinity mode) creates an anonymous account identified by a random ID. You choose a public username. We do not require or collect your name, email address, or phone number.

Gameplay data. We store your XP, scores, daily-challenge results, duel results, and which questions you have been served (to avoid repeats). This data is linked to your anonymous account.

Device data for ads. Our advertising partner (Google AdMob) may collect device identifiers (such as the advertising ID), approximate location derived from IP address, and app interaction data to serve and measure ads. We request non-personalized ads. See Google's Privacy Policy and AdMob data practices.

What we do NOT collect: contacts, photos, files, microphone or camera data, precise location, or any special-category personal data.

2. How data is stored

Online data is stored with Supabase (our database provider) on servers protected by row-level security: your data is only accessible to your own authenticated session. Offline progress (levels, local XP) is stored only on your device.

3. How data is used

Exclusively to operate the game: showing leaderboards, matching duels, tracking progress, preventing repeated questions, and serving ads that fund the free game. We do not sell personal data.

4. Data sharing

Your public username and XP appear on leaderboards visible to other players. Duel opponents see your username and duel score. Data processors we use: Supabase (database and authentication) and Google AdMob (advertising). No other sharing occurs.

5. Account and data deletion

You can permanently delete your account and all associated cloud data at any time, directly in the app: Profile → Delete account. Deletion is immediate and irreversible. Local device data can be removed by uninstalling the app or clearing the app's storage.

6. Children

MindRivals is a general-audience quiz game and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. Accounts are anonymous and no personal details are requested from any user.
